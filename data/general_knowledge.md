Q: What is Auxilium?
A: Auxilium is a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting students by connecting them with dedicated mentors. We facilitate one-on-one mentorship pairings and host academic webinars to help students navigate their educational and personal development journeys.

Q: Is there any cost to join?
A: No, Auxilium is entirely free. As a non-profit organization, our mission is to make mentorship and academic support accessible to all students, regardless of their financial situation.

Q: Where do the mentoring sessions take place?
A: All mentorship meetings and academic webinars are hosted virtually through our platform, allowing mentors and mentees to connect flexibly and securely from anywhere.

For Prospective Mentees
Q: How does the matching process work?
A: Once you sign up, you will fill out a brief profile detailing your academic goals, interests, and the areas where you need the most support. Our system and coordination team then carefully pair you with a mentor whose background and expertise align with your specific needs.

Q: What kind of help can I expect from my mentor?
A: Mentors provide a wide range of support, including academic guidance, study strategies, career advice, and general encouragement. You can also participate in our community-wide academic webinars for additional, structured learning opportunities.

Q: How often will I meet with my mentor?
A: We recommend that mentors and mentees meet at least once or twice a month, but the exact schedule is highly flexible. You and your mentor will decide on a meeting cadence that works best for both of your schedules.

Q: What if my mentor and I aren't a good fit?
A: We want to ensure every mentorship experience is positive and productive. If you feel your current pairing isn't working out, you can reach out to our Mentorship Coordination team, and we will happily facilitate a new match.

For Prospective Mentors
Q: How does the mentorship matching process work?
A: Our dedicated Mentorship Coordinators carefully pair mentors with mentees based on their specific needs and strengths. For example, a pre-IB student will be paired with a current IB student to receive highly relevant guidance throughout their high school journey. Our program focuses not just on academic lessons, but on guiding career decisions and building a strong work ethic.

Q: Who can become a mentor at Auxilium?
A: We welcome individuals who have a strong academic or professional background and a genuine desire to guide and support students. Whether you are an upper-year university student, a recent graduate, or an established professional, your experience is valuable.

Q: What is the time commitment required to mentor?
A: The standard commitment is 1–3 hours per month, which includes your one-on-one sessions and any brief preparation. We design the program to be highly flexible so it can fit around your personal and professional schedule.

Q: Do I need prior mentoring or teaching experience?
A: No prior formal experience is required! While previous teaching, tutoring, or leadership experience is a bonus, the most important qualities are empathy, strong communication skills, and a willingness to help a student succeed. We provide guidelines and resources to help you get started.

Q: Can I host or lead one of the academic webinars?
A: Absolutely. If you have a specific area of expertise, study methodology, or topic you'd like to share with a broader audience, we encourage our mentors to lead or participate in our regular academic webinars.

Our Story & Mission
Q: What is the history and meaning behind Auxilium?
A: "Auxilium" translates from Latin to "assist and support," which forms the entire foundation of our organization. We are a federally registered non-profit established to give back to our society. Our support programs grew out of a deep need to connect and assist people during the unprecedented challenges of the COVID-19 pandemic, combating loneliness and providing vital community care.

Q: What is the main mission of the organization?
A: Our mission is to support mental health and education in youth by fostering community involvement and leadership. We empower young people by providing volunteer opportunities, creating valuable connections across Ottawa, and producing accessible resources.

Q: What are Auxilium's core pillars?
A: Our work is built on two main pillars:

Education: We focus on equity in learning, informing the public about recent events, and training students to create a better learning environment for everyone.

Mental Health: We strive to ensure everyone lives a fulfilling life. We support our community with social events, interactive programs, and resources designed to raise awareness and create a highly inclusive environment.

The Team & Community
Q: Who leads Auxilium?
A: Auxilium is driven by a dedicated team of youth leaders and volunteers. The organization was established by our Founder, Allison Wang, and is led by our President, Sherilyn Wen. They are supported by a passionate internal team of Mentorship Coordinators, Math and Coding Circle Leads, Website Developers, and Social Media Officers.

Q: Who are the mentors in the program?
A: Our mentors are exceptional middle school and high school students based in the Ottawa area. To ensure high-quality guidance, mentors must maintain a grade point average of at least 85% in the subjects they teach. All mentors go through a thorough application process, which may include an interview or resume review.


Math & Coding Circles
Q: What is the Math Circle Program?
A: The Math Circle is a highly interactive, virtual program running from September to April. Each class features multiple instructors to ensure students receive immediate support. Whether getting quick responses in the main chat or jumping into a breakout room for personalized, one-on-one help, students are fully supported.

Q: What is the Coding Circle Program?
A: The Coding Circle is a 25-week virtual course running from September to January that introduces middle school students to computer science. Through fun, engaging, and interactive lessons, our goal is to foster a lifelong love for technology and innovation in young minds.

Q: How do I sign up for the next cycle of the Math or Coding Circles?
A: We are always preparing for our next cycle with fresh content and new instructors! To stay updated on registration dates and new sessions, be sure to follow our Instagram page.
